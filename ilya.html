Guild of Heroes: Pathfinder Corp

In the Guild's early days, quests were usually limited to rumors or whether commoners could reach the front desk fast enough. This wasn't an issue at the beginning, as the guild only had so much manpower in the chaotic days of old. But as the guild grew with more members and influence, so too did misinformation, fraud, and lack of trust around various quest givers. Quests started to report mismatching information and rewards due to a variety of reasons: commoner lack of knowledge, greedy merchants, villainous individuals, and even just no one being in the area to confirm. The Guild attempted to mitigate this through an informal system of familiars, tools, and magic, but it wasn't enough. The Wolford Stars Incident and subsequent Twilight Battle of Wolford forced the Guild to acknowledge the their lack of information gathering and processing capabilities. As such, the Pathfinder Corp was established.

The Pathfinder Corp was formed to cover the roles of intelligence gathering, quest verification, and regular scouting duties, however has since expanded to include exploring regions where guildmembers, and people in general, don't usually reach. Acting as the Guild's eyes and ears on the ground, pathfinders explore areas where the Guild can't through conventional means, gathering accurate information about possible quests and threats. They also act as a psuedo intelligence network in conjunction with various mercantile guilds.

Pathfinders exist within the same hierarchy of the Guild, but are afforded more privileges and trust in exchange for stricter requirements and duties. In essence, they act as mobile guild staff, able to officiate in matters corresponding to their rank. They also carry specialized items including observation tools, priority communication tools, and evacuation equipment. At minimum pathfinders must be able to fight and survive long enough to deliver information back to the Guild. Usually they will stay onsite to act as guides or informants for guild members who follow. There are also pathfinders who specialize in espionage activities, gathering information on nations and organizations hostile to the guild. But most pathfinders explore less traveled and unknown regions.

For guild members, they are the guides and quest givers. For hostile forces, they are the encroaching signs of unwanted interlopers. But for the many who despair, they are first signs of hope, the hope that the Heroes will come.

History
Every profession has its rumors; adventuring heroes have their tales of valor, enterprising merchants have their rumors of profit and wealth, scheming nobles have their gossip of ill tidings. For the working staff of various guild halls across Melicau, their whispers talk of those who walk through their doors. From rumors of celebrity appearances to ill mannered customers and enigmatic figures, all are not above the scrutiny of a staffer. However, few rumors are as widespread as the sightings of the Wandering Wizard, a figure who seems to be everywhere and nowhere at once, always in the wind. The small side stories her appearances accompany usually include a basic description of her figure and her book, a brief yet pleasant interaction, and the prologue to a much grander tale. To many, she is a mysterious traveler, walking across far reaching distances, appearing and disappearing at towns and villages for reasons unknown. But for the staff of the Guild of Heroes, she is their ally and friend among bureaucratic paperwork, the Pathfinder Ilya Reyes.

A brief search of recent years in the Guild Archive will create a timeline of Ilya Reyes’s long and storied wanderings. She joined the Guild at the age of 15, already exploring the world and carrying the arcane legacy of her family, an odd family with other mentions in the annals of history. Apparently she was found by an adventuring group of Heroes on their own quest and was convinced to join the Guild. Her own stated reason for joining is “if I’m going to be exploring the world, might as well do some good for this world.” She was immediately placed under the attention of an experienced Master rank Pathfinder, a spellweaver who once worked as the receptionist at the HQ of the Guild. Her apprenticeship under a Pathfinder was meant to only be a way to keep up with her wandering nature, but it turned out to be a boon for the Guild. Ilya could handle a variety of intelligence gathering and processing missions, and penned many quality reports to the guild. Within those reports shone a strong understanding of the arcane and a drive for exploration and learning. Documented interactions with her have shown her to maintain a pleasant and friendly nature, an inquisitive mind, and a love of exploration. Later interviews with Ilya exposed another motive for her movements: “to read all of my family’s compendium and to add my own words to it.”

Eventually a recommendation from her master and various staff members in the Guild led to her joining the Pathfinder Corp, subsequently followed by a promotion to Hero rank. At the age of 19, she began her travels independently once more, serving the Guild as one of its best Pathfinders. She has since started tales of her own, garnering a small reputation of reliability and assurance among both Guild members and staff. For those familiar with the Guild, her presence in paper is a common sight, and her presence in person is a rarity. Regardless, she remains a welcome part of the Guild of Heroes.

Appearance
Standing at 5 foot 7 inches, Ilya moves with the steady step of a traveler. From afar, the most noticeable characteristics are the compendium by her side, her large pointy hat, and her walking stick. To those who see with magical sight, she is surrounded by an aura of mana, a result of her near constant use when traveling. Her mild smile and overall pleasant demeanor leaves an overall positive impresson on those she encounters

Skills & Abilities
Wizard of the Reyes Family Line
Extremely familiar with the arcane. Carries the Reyes Compendium of the Arcane, a mix of spells from various regions, enabling a wide variety of arcane methods for any problem. Ilya has only understood half of the compendium, and must work to understand the rest and eventually add her own contributions to it.
Pathfinder Hero
A Guild of Heroes Pathfinder of Hero rank. Her long service and quality information reports have garnered her respect from within the Guild's administrative staff. She has enough combat capability to survive the most dire of circumstances, though her strength lies in information gathering and processing. Her nature to appear and disappear sporadically at various quests and guildhalls has spawned rumors of "The Wandering Wizard."
Wanderer
Seized by the family illness of wanderlust, Ilya regularly travels from place to place, exploring anything and everything. As such, she's learned to live off the land, strength her stamina, and learn various languages. Her travels has shown her the highs and lows of the world, exposed her to a variety of skills and professions, and made herself a small personal fortune. She moves with steady step, quickly through any terrain, and without tire, with a bit of magical help.
Trained Magic User
Early exposure to magic training, studious learning from her pathfinder predecessor, and near constant use of magic has trained her mastery and control over her abilities. Within her cohort of Heroes, she easily has one of the larger pools of mana, and fine grain control over it.
Magic
The Reyes Compendium of the Arcane
As the inheritor of the Reyes family Compendium, she has access to a wide array of magical spells and arcane knowledge. As long as she wields the compendium, she can draw out any number of spells without looking, as long as she can read past her ancestors ciphers and understand the arcane meaning behind it. The compendium contains spells from various different regions and draws upon various different schools of magic. The types of magic include but are not limited to elemental, life, spatial, healing, enchantment, illusions, etc.
Magic Vision and Farsight
Ilya is almost constantly using magic vision and farsight, alerting her to her surroundings.
Life Magic and Healing Magic
As a well seasoned traveler, Ilya has learned how to survive and thrive in the outdoors. Light, healing, water, cooking, cleaning, everything one would need to live the nomad life.
Enchantment Magic
Ilya almost always has enchantments that improve her traveling capabilities. This is usually strengthen, fleet foot, featherstep, and night vision.
Weaknesses
Close Quarter Combat
She knows enough staff arts and defensive spells to ward off attacks, but is by no means an expert at close quarter combat
Ambitions of the Arcane
Her goal and devotion to the studies of the Arcane have led her to some truly dire situations. Both “curiosity killed the cat” and “cats have nine lives” apply to this gal.
